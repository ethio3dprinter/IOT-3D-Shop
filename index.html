The purpose of 3D printer machine from users perspective (students of the campus)

Mechanical students

Printing makes it possible to produce objects with much more complex structures than traditional manufacturing methods. eg:machine elements prototypes can be printed or sometimes even the real parts.

Electrical students

Multi-material printing allows the development of embedded circuits in flexible substrates, or the development of novel 3D antenna designs. modern 3D printers can include both conductive and non-conductive materials.

Electromechanical students

To prototype parts of robots and automotive.

Civil students

Construction 3D printing may allow, faster and more accurate construction of complex or bespoke items as well as lowering labour costs and producing less waste. It might also enable construction to be undertaken in harsh or dangerous environments not suitable for a human workforce such as in space.

Hydraulics students

3D printing allows hydraulic components manufacturers to make parts lighter and smaller in size. Take a hydraulic manifold as an example. This part, which helps to regulate fluid flow between the components of a hydraulic system, is typically machined from a solid block of metal.
